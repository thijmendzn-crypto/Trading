# Trading Psychology

## The System is the Edge
Your edge is not in predicting the market. Your edge is in executing the system perfectly, every single time.

## The Three Killers of Edge
1. **FOMO** — Entering setups that don't fully qualify because you fear missing out
2. **Revenge trading** — Increasing size or forcing trades after a loss to recover
3. **Overconfidence** — Relaxing rules after a winning streak

## Pre-Trade Checklist (Mental)
Before every trade ask:
- Is this setup in my knowledge base?
- Am I entering because the system says so, or because I feel like it?
- If this trade loses, was it still correct to take? (process, not outcome)
- Am I within my daily/weekly risk limits?

## State-Based Rules
- If you have lost 2 trades in a row: mandatory 1-hour break
- If you are angry, stressed, or excited: do not trade
- If you are questioning the system: do not trade, review the knowledge base
- If you had a big winner: danger zone — you may become reckless

## Journal Discipline
After every trade, record:
- What was the setup?
- Did I follow the plan exactly?
- If not, what did I do differently and why?
- What would I do differently next time?

## Accountability Rules
- Never skip journaling a trade
- Review weekly statistics every Sunday
- Look for patterns in losing trades — are they all the same mistake?
- Celebrate consistent execution, not just profitable trades

## Long-Term Mindset
- Think in sample sizes of 50–100 trades, not individual outcomes
- A single losing trade means nothing if it was correctly executed
- Your goal is a positive expectancy system executed consistently
- Drawdowns are normal — the question is whether you stay within plan
