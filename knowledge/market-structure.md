# Market Structure

## Structure Definitions

### Bullish Structure (HH/HL)
- Higher High (HH): A swing high that exceeds the previous swing high
- Higher Low (HL): A swing low that stays above the previous swing low
- Confirmed bullish bias when both HH and HL are present

### Bearish Structure (LH/LL)
- Lower High (LH): A swing high that fails to reach the previous swing high
- Lower Low (LL): A swing low that breaks below the previous swing low
- Confirmed bearish bias when both LH and LL are present

### Break of Structure (BOS)
- Bullish BOS: Price breaks above the most recent swing high in a downtrend
- Bearish BOS: Price breaks below the most recent swing low in an uptrend
- BOS signals potential trend change — requires confirmation

### Change of Character (CHoCH)
- First indication of trend reversal
- In downtrend: price fails to make new LL and breaks above last LH
- In uptrend: price fails to make new HH and breaks below last HL
- CHoCH alone is not sufficient for entry — wait for structure confirmation

## Swing Point Identification
- Use at least 3 candles on each side to define a valid swing high/low
- Wick extremes count — not just candle bodies
- Identify on primary timeframe and confirm on higher timeframe

## Order Blocks
- The last up-candle before a significant bearish move (bearish OB)
- The last down-candle before a significant bullish move (bullish OB)
- Must have been the origin of a move that broke structure
- Valid until price returns and closes beyond it

## Fair Value Gaps (FVG / Imbalance)
- Three-candle pattern: candle 1 wick and candle 3 wick do not overlap
- Gap represents inefficiency — price tends to return to fill it
- Only trade FVGs in direction of overall trend
- 50% of the FVG is the ideal entry point

## Key Rules
- Always identify structure on at least 2 timeframes before entry
- Never trade against the higher timeframe structure
- A failed BOS (price breaks level then reverses) is a strong signal
- Structure is more important than any indicator
