# Exit Models

## Core Exit Philosophy
Plan your exit before you enter. An unplanned exit is an emotional exit.

## Standard Exit Structure

### Three-Target Model
- **TP1 (1.5R):** Take 40% of position — secures partial profit, removes pressure
- **TP2 (2.5R):** Take 40% of position — core profit target
- **TP3 (4R+):** Let 20% run — only if structure allows, trail stop

### Stop Management
- After TP1 hit: Move stop to breakeven immediately
- After TP2 hit: Trail stop at last swing low/high on entry timeframe
- Never move stop to a worse position once set

## Partial Exit Triggers
- Price rejects from next key resistance/support level
- Volume drops significantly (>50% below average)
- RSI reaches extreme (>75 or <25) on entry timeframe
- Major news event approaching within 1 hour

## Full Exit Triggers (Override TP targets)
- Market structure breaks against the trade direction
- Higher timeframe candle closes strongly against position
- Regime shifts to high volatility
- Daily loss limit approaching

## Time-Based Exit
- If a trade has not moved 1R in favor within 24 hours: exit 50%
- If still not moved in 48 hours: exit fully (time decay of edge)
- Day trades: close by end of session unless strong structure holds

## Common Exit Mistakes to Avoid
- Exiting at TP1 and not re-entering for TP2 (letting winners cut short)
- Moving stop too tight too quickly (getting shaken out)
- Holding through regime change (ignoring the system)
- Removing TP because "it feels like it will go further" (emotional)
- Not taking TP because "it might go higher" (greed)
