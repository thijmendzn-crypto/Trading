# Volume Analysis

## Volume Principles
Volume precedes price. Institutional activity shows in volume before price moves.

## Key Volume Signals

### Confirming Volume
- Breakout candle with >1.3x average volume: genuine breakout
- Trend candles with progressively increasing volume: strong trend
- Reversal candle with high volume spike: climax, potential reversal

### Warning Volume
- Breakout with below-average volume: likely false breakout
- New price high with declining volume: divergence, trend weakening
- Spike volume on a consolidation candle: absorption (potential reversal)

## Volume Indicators

### Volume Ratio
- Current candle volume / 20-period SMA of volume
- < 0.7: Low activity — avoid entries
- 0.7–1.2: Normal
- 1.2–2.0: Above average — good for confirmations
- > 2.0: Exceptional — climax or major event

### VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)
- Intraday fair value reference
- Institutional orders often cluster around VWAP
- Above VWAP in session: bulls paying up — bullish bias
- Below VWAP in session: bears in control — bearish bias
- First touch of VWAP after extended move: potential bounce/rejection

### Volume Profile (if available)
- Point of Control (POC): price level with most volume traded
- Value Area High/Low (VAH/VAL): 70% of volume traded within this range
- Low Volume Nodes (LVN): price passes through quickly — poor support/resistance
- High Volume Nodes (HVN): price consolidates — strong support/resistance

## Session Volume Patterns
- Pre-market/Asian session: lower volume, less reliable signals
- London open (08:00 UTC): volume spike — major directional moves begin
- London/NY overlap (13:00–16:00 UTC): highest volume of day — most reliable signals
- NY afternoon (17:00–21:00 UTC): declining volume — reduce new entries
- Weekends: 30–50% lower volume — avoid new positions
