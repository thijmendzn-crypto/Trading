# Indicators

## Philosophy
Indicators are confirmation tools, not prediction tools.
Price action and structure always take precedence.
Never use more than 4 indicators simultaneously.

## Primary Indicators

### ADX (Average Directional Index) — Period: 14
**Purpose:** Measure trend strength
- > 35: Very strong trend
- 25–35: Strong trend — good for continuation trades
- 20–25: Moderate trend — caution
- < 20: No trend — avoid trend trades

### EMA Stack — Periods: 20, 50, 200
**Purpose:** Trend direction and dynamic support/resistance
- Price > 20 EMA > 50 EMA > 200 EMA: Bullish alignment
- Price < 20 EMA < 50 EMA < 200 EMA: Bearish alignment
- EMAs converging: Transitioning regime — reduce exposure

### ATR (Average True Range) — Period: 14
**Purpose:** Measure volatility, set stops
- Use ATR to validate stop placement (minimum 0.5 ATR)
- ATR ratio (current/20-SMA): < 0.8 = low vol, > 2.0 = high vol (avoid)

### RSI — Period: 14
**Purpose:** Momentum and divergence detection
- Not used as overbought/oversold in trending markets
- Divergence: price makes new high/low but RSI does not = momentum weakening
- RSI < 40 in uptrend pullback = value entry zone
- RSI > 60 in downtrend pullback = value short zone

### VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)
**Purpose:** Intraday value area and institutional reference
- Price above VWAP: buyers in control for the session
- Price below VWAP: sellers in control
- Reversion to VWAP in trending market = pullback entry opportunity
- Only use for intraday timeframes (1M, 5M, 15M, 1H)

## Volume
**Purpose:** Confirm conviction behind moves
- Breakout requires >1.3x average volume
- Pullback should have <0.8x average volume (shows lack of selling pressure)
- Volume spike on reversal candle = strong confirmation

## What NOT to Use
- MACD as a signal generator (too lagging)
- Stochastic in trending markets
- Bollinger Bands as entry signals alone
- Any indicator with more than 3 parameters to optimize (overfitting risk)

## Indicator Hierarchy
1. Price action and structure (primary)
2. EMA alignment and VWAP (trend context)
3. ADX (regime confirmation)
4. Volume (conviction filter)
5. RSI divergence (timing)
6. ATR (risk sizing only)
